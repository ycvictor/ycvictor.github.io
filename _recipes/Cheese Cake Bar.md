## Cheese Cake Bar

A short break from debugging turned into finally making something I’d been wanting to try for a while. While stuck on a distributed system bug, I stepped away and baked instead and it turned out really well. It’s the kind of treat that’s fun to share and the whole process felt like a reset with no phone, full focus, and something tasty to enjoy at the end.
<br><br>

### Ingredients
<label><input type="checkbox"> 160g biscuits or graham crackers</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 80g salted butter, melted</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 500g cream cheese, room temp</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 140g sugar</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 1 tsp vanilla</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 2 large eggs, room temp</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 7g cornstarch</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 1/4 tsp salt</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 80ml heavy cream</label><br>

**Strawberry topping**  
<label><input type="checkbox"> 300g strawberries, chopped</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 50g sugar</label><br>

**Crumble topping**  
<label><input type="checkbox"> 100g flour</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 65g light brown sugar</label><br>
<label><input type="checkbox"> 65g unsalted butter</label><br>

### Procedure
1. For the topping, heat half the strawberries with sugar until syrupy, simmer until thick, cool, then mix in the remaining strawberries.  
2. Heat oven to 180°C (350°F). Line and grease an 8×8 dish.  
3. Crush biscuits, mix with melted butter, press firmly into the pan, and chill.  
    <figure>
    <img src="/images/recipes/Cheese Cake Bar-crush biscuits.png" alt="crush biscuits" class="recipe-image">
    </figure>
4. Soften cream cheese, mix in sugar, vanilla, eggs, cornstarch, salt, and cream until smooth.  
5. Pour over the base and bake 35–40 minutes until set but wobbly.  
    <figure>
    <img src="/images/recipes/Cheese Cake Bar-cheese cake.jpeg" alt="cheese cake" class="recipe-image">
    </figure>
6. Combine flour and brown sugar, rub in butter to form crumbles.  
    <figure>
    <img src="/images/recipes/Cheese Cake Bar-crumble.png" alt="crumble" class="recipe-image">
    </figure>

7. Spread cooled strawberry topping over the cheesecake, add crumble, and bake 20 minutes until golden.  
    <figure>
    <img src="/images/recipes/Cheese Cake Bar-after baking.png" alt="after baking" class="recipe-image">
    </figure>
8. Cool briefly, chill for several hours or overnight, lift out, slice, and serve.

        </figure>

        </figure>
    </div>
    
## Tips
- Next time, add a bit more salt to the crust for better contrast.
- The blueberry flavor was faint this round; consider increasing the amount or using a more concentrated form.
